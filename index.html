Alan Turing: A tribute to his life and achievements that paved the way for modern computer science.

Codebreaker and pioneer of artificial intelligence.

Here is a short time line of Alan Turing's life and achievements:

1912

Alan Mathison Turing was born in Maida Vale, London.

1918

Turing joins St Michael's day school in Hastings, UK.

1922

Turing is sent to Hazelhurst Preparatory School, UK and learns to play chess.

1926

TAged 14, he was sent to Sherborne School in Dorset.

1931

Turing goes to study Mathematics at King's College, Cambridge.

1935

Turing proves the central limit theorem in his dissertation and is made a fellow at King's College, Cambridge.

1936

Turing published his paper On Computable Numbers and an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem (decision problem).

1936-1938

Turing went to the Institute of Advanced Studies at Princeton, studying under Alonzo Church. There he started to study cryptology and mathematics.

1938

Turing started to work part-time at the Government Code and Cypher School, Cheltenham, UK.

1939

Turing works with Gordon Welchman to develop the Bombe, a device for decrypting the messages sent by Germans using their Enigma machines.

1941

Turing proposed marriage to Hut 8 co-worker Joan Clarke, a fellow mathematician and cryptanalyst, but their engagement was short-lived. After admitting his homosexuality to his fiancée, Turing decided that he could not go through with the marriage.

1942

Turing and his colleagues break the more complicated German Naval Enigma system. Turing travelled to the United States in November and worked with U.S. Navy cryptanalysts on Naval Enigma and bombe construction in Washington.

1943

Turing is asked to work as a top level intelligence link with USA, which he visits to share information on cryptology (code-breaking). Works at Bell Laboratories on speech encypherment.

1945

At the end of World War Two, Turing is awarded an OBE for his services to his country. Turing joined the National Physical Laboratory where he worked on developing an electronic digital stored-program computing machine that would later become the ACE .

1946

Published the first detailed design of a stored-program computer. Turing and his group pioneer modern computer programming, writing a library of sophisticated programs for the unbuilt ACE.

1947

Turing returned to Cambridge for a sabbatical year. The ACE was built in his absence and executed its first program on 10 May 1950.

1949

Turing became deputy director of the Computing Laboratory at Manchester University, working on software for one of the earliest stored program computers: the Manchester Mark 1. He also explored the problem of artificial intelligence and proposed an experiment that attempted to define a standard for machine intelligence, which would later become known as the Turing test.

1952

Turing is arrested for gross indecency and loses his security clearance. Turing's homosexuality resulted in a criminal prosecution in 1952, when homosexual acts were still illegal in the United Kingdom. He is offered chemical treatment as an alternative to imprisonment. The hormone treatment has a very detrimental effect on him.

1953

Turing publishes his classic paper on computer chess.

1954

Dies of cyanide poisoning June 7 in Wilmslow, Cheshire, England. June 8th, Turing's body is found in his home in Wilmslow, Cheshire. The post-mortem finds that his death had been caused by cyanide poisoning. His body is cremated at Woking crematorium.

"We can only see a short distance ahead, but we can see plenty there that needs to be done."

-- Alan Turing
